[[!meta title="⚠ Identity ⚠"]]

Tails alone cannot protect your identity in all cases. It is important that you also take steps to protect your identity.

Here are a couple of examples of what you can do to protect your identity:

Clean metadata from files before sharing them

Many file formats contain hidden data, or metadata:

  • Files created by word processing software often contain information about the author of the document, and the date and time the document was created.
  • JPEG and other image files often contain information about when and where a picture was taken, along with information about the manufacturer and model of the device used to take the picture.

To help you clean metadata from your files, Tails comes with MAT2, a metadata removal tool that supports a wide range of commonly used file formats.

Use Tails sessions for only one purpose at a time

If you use Tails sessions for more than one purpose at a time, an adversary could correlate a relationship between your activities.

For example, if you log into different accounts on the same website in a single Tails session, the website could determine that the accounts are used by the same person, since the connections are coming from the same Tor circuit.

To help prevent an adversary from correlating a relationship between the activities you perform while using Tails, you can reboot Tails between performing your different activities.

If you are concerned that the files you have saved in your Persistent Storage can be used to link your different activities together, you should consider using a different Tails USB stick for each of your activities.
