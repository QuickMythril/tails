Protecting your identity when using Tails

[[!inline pages="doc/about/warning/identity.inline" raw="yes" sort="age"]]

Clean metadata from files before sharing them

Many file formats contain hidden data, or metadata:

  • JPEG and other image files often contain information about where a picture was taken and information about the device used to take the picture.

  • Files created by word processing software often contain information about the author of the document, and the date and time the document was created.

To help you clean metadata from your files, Tails comes with MAT2, a metadata removal tool that supports a wide range of commonly used file formats.

Metadata has been used in the past to locate people from pictures they took. For an example, see NPR: Betrayed by metadata, John McAfee admits he's really in Guatemala.

Use Tails sessions for only one purpose at a time

If you use Tails sessions for more than one purpose at a time, an adversary could correlate a relationship between your activities.

For example, if you log into different accounts on the same website in a single Tails session, the website could determine that the accounts are used by the same person, since the connections are coming from the same Tor circuit.

We are not aware of any such attacks to deanonymize people online who used Tails for different purposes at a time.

If you are concerned that the files you have saved in your Persistent Storage can be used to link your different activities together, you should consider using a different Tails USB stick for each of your activities. For example, you can use one Tails USB stick for activism work and one Tails USB stick for journalism work.
